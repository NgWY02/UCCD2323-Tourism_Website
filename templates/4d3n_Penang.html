George Town: Street Art & Heritage

Start your Penang journey in the heart of George Town, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, and immerse yourself in its vibrant street art scene and rich cultural heritage.

  • Must-Visit:
    • Street Art: Wander through the streets and alleys to discover captivating murals and interactive installations.
    • Clan Jetties: Explore these unique waterfront settlements, remnants of Penang's past.
    • Fort Cornwallis: Visit this historic fort, the largest standing fort in Malaysia.
    • Kek Lok Si Temple: Admire the grandeur of this Buddhist temple complex, one of the largest in Southeast Asia.

  • Activities:
    • Penang Street Food Tour: Indulge in a culinary adventure and sample a variety of local delicacies.
    • Trishaw Ride: Take a leisurely trishaw ride through the historic streets of George Town.
    • Visit the Blue Mansion: Explore this beautifully restored Peranakan mansion and learn about its history.
2
Penang Hill & Batu Ferringhi

Escape the city heat and ascend Penang Hill for breathtaking views and cool mountain air. Then, head to Batu Ferringhi for some beachside relaxation.

  • Highlights:
    • Penang Hill: Take the funicular train up to Penang Hill and enjoy panoramic views of the island.
    • The Habitat Penang Hill: Explore this eco-tourism attraction with its canopy walk, treetop walk, and nature trails.
    • Batu Ferringhi Beach: Relax on the sandy shores, swim in the sea, or try water sports activities.
    • Tropical Spice Garden: Discover a variety of spices and herbs in this beautifully landscaped garden.

  • Ticket Prices:
    • Penang Hill Funicular Train: Varies depending on package; check their website for details.
    • The Habitat Penang Hill: Adult - RM50, Child - RM30
    • Tropical Spice Garden: Adult - RM39, Child - RM25
3
Culinary Adventures & Street Food

Embark on a culinary journey through Penang, savoring its diverse flavors and iconic street food.

  • Must-Try Dishes:
    • Asam Laksa: A tangy and spicy fish noodle soup, a Penang specialty.
    • Char Kway Teow: Stir-fried flat rice noodles with prawns, cockles, and bean sprouts.
    • Hokkien Mee: Prawn noodle soup, a flavorful and comforting dish.
    • Nasi Kandar: A popular rice dish with a variety of curries and side dishes.

  • Food Trails:
    • Gurney Drive Hawker Centre: A bustling food paradise with a wide selection of local dishes.
    • Chulia Street Night Market: Experience the vibrant night market scene and sample street food.
    • Explore local coffee shops (kopitiams): Enjoy traditional breakfast and coffee at these charming establishments.
4
Balik Pulau & Escape Theme Park

Venture to the quieter side of Penang and experience the natural beauty of Balik Pulau, then have some fun at Escape Theme Park.

  • Highlights:
    • Balik Pulau: Explore the charming countryside, visit durian orchards, and enjoy the scenic views.
    • Escape Theme Park: Have an adrenaline-pumping day at this outdoor adventure park with ziplines, obstacle courses, and more.

  • Tips:
    • Check the durian season if you're interested in tasting this unique fruit.
    • Book your tickets for Escape Theme Park in advance, especially during weekends and holidays.
    • Wear comfortable clothes and shoes for outdoor activities.
