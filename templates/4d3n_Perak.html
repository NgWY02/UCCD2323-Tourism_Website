Ipoh Old Town & Culinary Delights

Immerse yourself in the charm of Ipoh's Old Town, a treasure trove of historical buildings, vibrant street art, and mouthwatering culinary delights.

  • Must-Visit:
    • Concubine Lane: Wander through this narrow alleyway lined with shops and cafes, offering a glimpse into Ipoh's past.
    • Birch Memorial Clock Tower: Admire this iconic landmark, a tribute to a British Resident of Perak.
    • Ipoh Railway Station: Marvel at the grandeur of this Moorish-style building, often called the "Taj Mahal of Ipoh".

  • Culinary Delights:
    • Ipoh White Coffee: Savor the city's signature coffee, known for its smooth and aromatic flavor.
    • Nasi Ganja: Indulge in this flavorful rice dish, a local favorite with a cult following.
    • Ipoh Hor Fun: Try this silky smooth rice noodle soup, a must-try for any visitor to Ipoh.
2
Taiping's Heritage & Nature

Step back in time in Taiping, the first town established by the British in Peninsular Malaysia.

  • Must-Visit:
    • Taiping Lake Gardens: Enjoy a leisurely stroll through this picturesque park, home to a variety of flora and fauna.
    • Perak Museum: Delve into Perak's history and culture at this informative museum.
    • Zoo Taiping & Night Safari: Encounter a diverse range of animals at this well-maintained zoo, and experience the thrill of a night safari.

  • Ticket Prices:
    • Taiping Lake Gardens: Free admission
    • Perak Museum: Adult - RM5, Child - RM2
    • Zoo Taiping & Night Safari: Varies depending on package; check their website for details.
3
Adventure in Gua Tempurung

Embark on a thrilling caving adventure in Gua Tempurung, one of the largest limestone caves in Peninsular Malaysia.

  • Highlights:
    • Explore the cave's chambers and underground rivers.
    • Marvel at the stunning stalactites and stalagmites.
    • Choose from various tour packages based on your fitness level and interests.

  • Ticket Prices:
    • Varies depending on the chosen tour package.

  • Tips:
    • Wear comfortable shoes and clothing suitable for caving.
    • Bring a change of clothes and a towel.
    • Book your tour in advance, especially during peak seasons.
4
Relaxation on Pulau Pangkor

Unwind and rejuvenate on the idyllic Pulau Pangkor, a picturesque island off the coast of Perak.

  • Things to Do:
    • Soak up the sun on the pristine beaches like Pasir Bogak and Teluk Nipah.
    • Explore the island's natural beauty by hiking or renting a scooter.
    • Visit the historic Dutch Fort, a reminder of the island's colonial past.
    • Enjoy water activities like snorkeling, kayaking, and island hopping.

  • Tips:
    • Book your accommodation in advance, especially during peak seasons.
    • Bring sunscreen, insect repellent, and a hat.
    • Try the local seafood, especially the grilled fish and prawns.
